Tourist Spots in Malaysia

Petronas Twin Tower

built in 1998, are the tallest double structure in the world. The 88-story towers were once the tallest building in the world. Designed by architect Cesar Pelli in collaboration with Malaysia's government, the towers have a tube-in-tube high-strength concrete structure for extra stability.

Batu Cave

a 325-m tall mogote with a series of limestone caves in Gombak, Selangor, Malaysia. It is located about 13 km (8.1 mi) north of the capital city of Kuala Lumpur. The cave complex contains many Hindu temples, the most popular of which is a shrine dedicated to the deity Murugan. It is the focal point of the Tamil Hindu festival of Thaipusam in Malaysia. The complex also hosts a 43 m (141 ft) high Murugan statue, one of the largest Murugan statues in the world.

Paronama langkawi skycab

a 15-minute cable car ride on Langkawi Island, Malaysia, that takes you to the summit of Mount Machinchang, offering panoramic views of the surrounding area, including the Andaman Sea and the island's rainforest. The SkyCab also provides access to the famous Langkawi Sky Bridge, a curved suspension bridge.
